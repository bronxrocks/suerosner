Bobby Darin

Photo courtesy of Dodd Darin & bobbydarin.net. Used with permission.

Bobby Darin was born Walden Robert Cassotto in Bellevue Hospital, New York City, on May 14, 1936. As a child, he suffered from rheumatic fever, which left him with a seriously weakend heart. When he was around 6 or 7, he overheard a doctor saying that he didn't expect the child to live a long life. Knowing this, his ambition was to become a legend by the time he was 25.

By the time he was a teenager, he could play several instruments, including piano, drums, and guitar. He later added harmonica and xylophone. He never seriously considered a career in music until after he enrolled at Hunter College. He left Hunter after a couple of semesters, and got a job first as a demo writer then as a demo singer at the legendary Brill Building in New York City.

Darin's career took off in 1958 when he recorded Splish Splash, a song that Darin says he wrote in 12 minutes. The hits followed with Queen of the Hop (#9) and Dream Lover (#2). His biggest success would come the following year with Mack the Knife. It spent nine weeks at #1 and sold 2 million copies. The song became his signature song and would earn a Grammy for Record of the Year, while Darin won the Grammy for Best New Artist. The song also reflected on Darin's desire to appeal to a more mature audience.

His next hit, Beyond the Sea, had a swinging, big bandâ€“style arrangement, which helped Darin make the transition to performing into the nightclub circuit. He set an attendance record at the famous Copacabana in New York City and began playing casinos in Las Vegas.

While maintaining his successful music career, Darin branched out to movies. In 1960. He appeared in Come September, where he met actress Sandra Dee. The two went on to marry. They had one son, Dodd, born in 1961. Darin and Dee would divorce in 1967. Darin would go on to appear in about 10 films.

Darin died on December 20, 1973, following open heart surgery to repair two artificial heart valves. He was just 37 years old.

Darin's numerous career awards include
  • Grammy award, Record of the Year Mack the Knife
  • Grammy award, Best New Artist 1959
  • Golden Globe award, Best New Actor, Come September, 1962
  • Academy Award nominee, Best Supporting Actor, Captain Newman, MD, 1964
  • Induction into the Songwriters Hall of Fame in 1999.
  • Bobby Darin Day proclaimed in the Bronx, by the authority of Bronx Borough President, Fernando Ferrer
  • 2010 Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award
  • Street sign named in his honor in the Bronx Walk of Fame in 2004.
